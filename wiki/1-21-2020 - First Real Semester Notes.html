Anyway, what was I talking about?

To work towards check-in in a week (and the 7 minute presentation on Feb 10, week 5!), I want to try coding out functions in the play space, get things feeling right there (I have some ideas on making that work, but it'll all take experimentation of course.

Just had a thought... How much of the game's mechanics do I want to really be a part of the world? I thought of a way to do it via the chat, but what about other things? Should the replayable nature of it come into play at all? Do it like Reigns, where it is designed for you to go in over and over and evolve over time? A Groundhog day situation, almost like a ROGUELIKE, where you don't have all the pieces to solve the real issue the first time?

Holy shit. Did I just stumble on the ultimate form of this? Is this a fucking roguelike text adventure?! What a catchy genre sound... Oh man, because then you would be compelled by more than just curiosity to plow through the multiple ways to play the game, becoming armed with new information, some trial and error, where even failures yield important results.

Time play would have to be essential then for this to really make sense. Roguelikes tend to build their replayability into the story/mechanics unless they're very arcady. But not this one... This would need to have purpose. Does the black hole cause time distortions? And they're triggered by something that happens towards the end, except when you've got everything lined up, it doesn't... I need an explanation for this.

(There's some Outer Wilds bullshit going on here actually...)

Perhaps... something happened with the black hole causing regularly spaced time distortions that send certain things back like 15 minutes (to the start of things). You have quantum computing so your notes and info can remain the same, letting your knowledge persist between sessions, but the AI does not? It must have to reset, but you can mess with it in different ways to get different information out of it and such to learn how to escape this...

This is a really fun idea... but is rather ambitious. Perhaps too much so. I may still be able to keep it in some form, but need to be toned down probably. I really need to figure that out though! It's kind of important for figuring out the actual mechanics and how things work and shit! Perhaps save the time manipulation for like, the sequel...
